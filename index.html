Common Electrical Formulas

Ohm's Law

  • V = I × R (Voltage = Current × Resistance)
  • I = V / R (Current = Voltage / Resistance)
  • R = V / I (Resistance = Voltage / Current)

Power (DC / Single-Phase AC)

  • P = V × I (Power = Voltage × Current)
  • P = I² × R (Power = Current² × Resistance)
  • P = V² / R (Power = Voltage² / Resistance)

Wire Resistance

  • R = ρ × L / A
  • (Resistance = Resistivity × Length / Area)
  • Note: Ensure units match (Ω·m, m, m²)

Three-Phase Power (Balanced Load)

  • P (W) = √3 × VL-L × IL × PF (Real Power)
  • S (VA) = √3 × VL-L × IL (Apparent Power)
  • PF = P / S (Power Factor)
  • VL-L = Line-to-Line Voltage, IL = Line Current

Approximate Voltage Drop (VD)

  • Single Phase: VD = 2 × I × R = 2 × I × ρ × L / A
  • Three Phase: VD = √3 × I × R = √3 × I × ρ × L / A
  • Where R is the resistance of one conductor (ρ×L/A).
  • Percentage VD = (VD / Vsource) × 100%
